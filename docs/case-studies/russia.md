# روسیه

_transition پسا شوروی روسیه (1989–1999) یک مورد نادر از فروپاشی سیستماتیک دولت را نشان می‌دهد که بعداً با تلاش برای دموکراتیزاسیون در دوران یلتسین (1989–1999) دنبال شد.  تدوین قانون اساسی در حین جنگ داخلی (چечن)، تورم بالا و تقریباً انحلال فدراسیون رخ داد.  قانون اساسی سال 1993 سعی کرد یک سیستم ریاست‌جمهوری با خودمختاری منطقه‌ای را برقرار کند، اما نهادهای ضعیف، گروگان‌گیری ارباب‌رعایا و غلبه بخش امنیتی از تثبیت آن جلوگیری کردند.  ورود پوتین (2000) یک تغییر به سمت دموکراسی رقابتی را نشان داد که چگونگی بازگشت از انتقال‌های دموکراتیک ناتمام اگر نهادها قدرت اجرایی را محدود نکنند، را نشان می‌دهد.  تجربه روسیه به ویژه برای ایران در دوران پسا امپریالیستی با پیامدهای امنیتی هسته‌ای و منطقه‌ای مهم است.

## شباهت ساختاری به ایران

Russia's post-Soviet transition (1989–1999) represents a rare case of systemic state collapse followed by attempted democratization under Yeltsin (1989–1999). Constitutional drafting occurred amid civil conflict (Chechnya), hyperinflation, and the near-dissolution of the federation. The 1993 Constitution attempted to establish a presidential system with regional autonomy, but weak institutions, oligarchic capture, and security sector dominance prevented consolidation.

Putin's arrival (2000) marked a shift toward competitive authoritarianism, demonstrating how incomplete democratic transitions can regress if institutions fail to constrain executive power. Russia's experience is particularly relevant for Iran as a post-empire transition with nuclear and regional security implications.

## تشابه ساختاری با ایران

Russia's experience is similar to Iran's post-revolutionary transition (1979–1988) in several ways. Both countries faced a systemic state collapse, followed by an attempted democratization under Khomeini (1979–1988). Constitutional drafting occurred amid civil conflict (Iran-Iraq War), hyperinflation, and the near-dissolution of the monarchy. The 1979 Constitution attempted to establish a theocratic system with regional autonomy, but weak institutions, clerical capture, and security sector dominance prevented consolidation.

Putin's arrival in Russia marked a shift toward competitive authoritarianism, similar to Khamenei's rise to power in Iran. Both leaders demonstrated how incomplete democratic transitions can regress if institutions fail to constrain executive power. Russia's experience is particularly relevant for Iran as a post-empire transition with nuclear and regional security implications.

## تحلیل تطبیقی

A comparative analysis of Russia and Iran's post-transition experiences reveals several similarities and differences. Both countries faced a systemic state collapse, followed by an attempted democratization under a charismatic leader (Yeltsin in Russia and Khomeini in Iran). However, the outcomes were different: Russia transitioned to competitive authoritarianism, while Iran became a theocratic republic.

The 1993 Constitution in Russia and the 1979 Constitution in Iran share similarities in their attempts to establish a presidential system with regional autonomy. However, weak institutions, oligarchic capture, and security sector dominance prevented consolidation in both countries.

Putin's arrival in Russia marked a shift toward competitive authoritarianism, similar to Khamenei's rise to power in Iran. Both leaders demonstrated how incomplete democratic transitions can regress if institutions fail to constrain executive power. Russia's experience is particularly relevant for Iran as a post-empire transition with nuclear and regional security implications.

## مطالعه موردی

Russia's case study provides valuable insights into the challenges of democratization in post-transition countries. The country's experience highlights the importance of strong institutions, rule of law, and governance in preventing the regression of democratic transitions.

The case study also demonstrates the significance of path dependence in shaping a country's development pathways. Russia's transition to competitive authoritarianism was influenced by its post-Soviet legacy, which created a power vacuum that allowed Putin to consolidate power.

In conclusion, Russia's experience provides a valuable lesson for Iran and other post-transition countries: the importance of building strong institutions, promoting governance, and ensuring the rule of law in preventing the regression of democratic transitions.
