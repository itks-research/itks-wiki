# Sanctions relief → economic dividend → democratic mandate

**Domain:** Economic
**Framework Node:** L4.1
**Evidence Sources:** 0

## Description

When international sanctions are lifted as part of transitional bargains, the resulting economic recovery can increase government resources for social spending and public sector employment. This visible economic improvement can reinforce democratic reforms by creating constituencies (youth, workers, public sector) that benefit from peace and democratic governance, hardening support for anti-authoritarian coalitions.

## Context Requirements

- **Sanctions Regime:** comprehensive_with_large_economic_cost
- **Sanctions Relief Conditionality:** linked_to_democratic_reforms
- **Pent Up Investment Demand:** high_eager_to_rebuild
- **Economic Multiplier Effects:** rapid_growth_visible
- **Political Coalition Benefits:** distributed_to_coalition_supporters

## Expected Outcomes

Economic growth; improved living standards; expanded democratic constituencies; deepened commitment to democratic settlement

## Typical Triggers

- International agreement lifting comprehensive sanctions
- Foreign direct investment floods into economy
- Government revenue increases enabling wage increases
- Consumer prices fall as imports become available
- Youth employment improves in newly opened sectors

**Typical Timeframe:** 24 months
