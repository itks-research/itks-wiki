# Youth bulge → protest escalation cycle

**Domain:** Social
**Framework Node:** L2.2
**Evidence Sources:** 0

## Description

When large youth populations (35%+ under 25) face unemployment and blocked social mobility, they become volatile protest bases. Youth-led movements can escalate rapidly through social media coordination, sustain prolonged campaigns, and respond to repression with renewed mobilization. This demographic mechanism explains both protest intensity and regime vulnerability to generational opposition.

## Context Requirements

- **Youth Share Of Population:** 35_percent_or_higher
- **Youth Unemployment:** high_relative_to_adult
- **Educational Access:** high_literacy_and_education
- **Digital Connectivity:** high_social_media_penetration
- **Migration Options:** limited_exit_options_for_youth

## Expected Outcomes

Sustained protest mobilization; escalation cycles; regime vulnerability to youth-led movements; generational politicization

## Typical Triggers

- Youth unemployment spike after economic shock
- Social media campaign goes viral among youth
- Visible police violence against young protesters
- University closures or exam cancellations
- Youth arrests or conscription threats

**Typical Timeframe:** 18 months
