# Economic shock therapy → social instability → backsliding

**Domain:** Economic
**Framework Node:** L4.2
**Evidence Sources:** 0

## Description

Rapid macroeconomic stabilization or structural adjustment programs create severe short-term social costs that destabilize democracies. This mechanism explains why some transitions fail after liberalization: when economic shocks (price spikes, unemployment, currency collapse) precede institution-building, mass discontent fuels authoritarian backlash or coup attempts before democratic institutions can consolidate.

## Context Requirements

- **Inflation Rate:** moderate_to_hyperinflation
- **Unemployment Surge:** sudden_5pct_plus
- **Institutional Strength:** weak_nascent_democracy
- **Time To Institution Building:** months_not_years
- **Social Safety Net:** degraded_or_absent

## Expected Outcomes

Democratic consolidation failure; authoritarian revival; polarization and political instability

## Typical Triggers

- IMF-mandated subsidy removal
- Currency devaluation or hyperinflation
- Collapse of major state enterprise
- Mass layoffs in public sector
- Rapid decline in purchasing power

**Typical Timeframe:** 24 months
