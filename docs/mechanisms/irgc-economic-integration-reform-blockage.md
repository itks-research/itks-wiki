# IRGC economic integration → reform blockage

**Domain:** Iran Specific
**Framework Node:** L2.1
**Evidence Sources:** 0

## Description

When the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) controls major economic assets (ports, energy, manufacturing, construction), it can veto reforms that threaten its commercial interests. This Iran-specific mechanism explains how a security organization becomes a structural impediment to liberalization: IRGC commanders block privatization, oppose foreign investment, and leverage economic leverage to maintain security dominance.

## Context Requirements

- **Irgc Economic Share:** 15_percent_plus_of_economy
- **Irgc Asset Concentration:** strategic_sectors_ports_energy
- **Regime Financial Dependence:** significant_IRGC_revenue
- **Parallel Security State:** insulated_from_civilian_oversight
- **Factional Elite Balance:** IRGC_not_weakened

## Expected Outcomes

Blocked economic reform; perpetuated corruption; IRGC veto over democratization; stalled transition

## Typical Triggers

- Opposition demands transparency in IRGC holdings
- Reformist president tries to reduce IRGC role
- International pressure for sanctions relief conditional on reform
- Newly visible IRGC assets provoke public backlash
- Youth movements demand competitive markets

**Typical Timeframe:** 60 months
