# Diaspora capital → transition acceleration

**Domain:** Social
**Framework Node:** L4.4
**Evidence Sources:** 0

## Description

Large diaspora communities with resources, international networks, and democratic experience can accelerate transitions by providing capital, technical expertise, and international legitimacy to opposition movements and transitional institutions. Diaspora money for media, legal defense, and institution-building bypasses sanctions and regime resource controls.

## Context Requirements

- **Diaspora Size:** large_relative_to_origin
- **Diaspora Resources:** significant_capital_and_networks
- **Diaspora Organization:** coordinated_at_least_partially
- **Host Country Tolerance:** permissive_of_diaspora_activity
- **Domestic Absorption Capacity:** present_for_diaspora_capital

## Expected Outcomes

Enhanced opposition financial capacity; faster institution-building; stronger international civil society linkages

## Typical Triggers

- Sanctions restrict regime's overseas financial access
- Democratic movements seek international support
- Diaspora political organizations mobilize homeland networks
- Opposition groups establish international NGOs
- Exiled intellectuals advise transitional institutions

**Typical Timeframe:** 12 months
