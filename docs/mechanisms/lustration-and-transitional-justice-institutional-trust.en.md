# Lustration and transitional justice → institutional trust

**Domain:** Institutional
**Framework Node:** L3.3
**Evidence Sources:** 0

## Description

When transitions establish credible transitional justice mechanisms (truth commissions, lustration laws, criminal prosecutions for security crimes), they can rebuild public trust in institutions that were used for oppression. This mechanism works by making clear breaks with the old regime's practices and signaling that new institutions will not replicate authoritarianism.

## Context Requirements

- **Past Regime Crimes:** documented_and_known_to_public
- **International Legal Precedent:** available_for_reference
- **Judicial Independence:** sufficient_to_credibly_prosecute
- **Transitional Justice Consensus:** broad_political_agreement
- **Victim Compensation Capacity:** adequate_resources_allocated

## Expected Outcomes

Increased institutional legitimacy; public confidence in post-transition courts and security services; reduced cycles of revenge

## Typical Triggers

- Victims' groups demand accountability for past crimes
- International pressure for truth commission
- Public disclosure of security archives
- First prosecutions of regime officials
- Truth commission findings released and debated

**Typical Timeframe:** 36 months
