# Party system institutionalization → democratic stability

**Domain:** Political
**Framework Node:** L4.3
**Evidence Sources:** 0

## Description

When political parties develop stable bases, internal discipline, organizational persistence across elections, and commitment to democratic rules, they create predictable political competition that depolarizes politics. Institutionalized parties are less dependent on individual strongmen and more invested in democratic procedures that constrain them but also limit opponents.

## Context Requirements

- **Party Organizational Depth:** local_branches_and_members
- **Electoral Stability:** core_constituency_loyalty
- **Party Internal Discipline:** leadership_can_enforce_rules
- **Interparty Moderation:** competition_within_democratic_bounds
- **Electoral System Stability:** rules_favor_party_institutionalization

## Expected Outcomes

Depolarized politics; reduced personalism and strongmanism; democratic stability through regularized competition

## Typical Triggers

- Multiple electoral cycles establish party loyalties
- Parties develop organizational machinery in regions
- Internal party democracy becomes expected
- Party platforms differentiate but stay within democracy
- Youth and women integrate into party structures

**Typical Timeframe:** 60 months
