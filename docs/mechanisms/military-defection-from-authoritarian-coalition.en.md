# Military defection from authoritarian coalition

**Domain:** Security
**Framework Node:** L2.1
**Evidence Sources:** 0

## Description

When military factions defect from authoritarian security establishments, they eliminate the regime's monopoly on legitimate force. This mechanism is critical for transitions because militaries typically serve as the regime's enforcement backbone; their withdrawal or split creates a credibility gap and opens space for non-violent opposition to organize without fear of violent suppression.

## Context Requirements

- **Regime Type:** authoritarian_military_dominated
- **Military Cohesion:** fragmented
- **External Pressure:** moderate_to_high
- **Internal Factional Conflict:** present
- **Elite Legitimacy Crisis:** active

## Expected Outcomes

Demonstrable defection or neutrality by military units; reduced regime capacity to suppress opposition; transition window opens

## Typical Triggers

- Massive opposition protests with military casualties
- Ideological schism within officer corps
- Economic collapse affecting military salaries
- International sanctions on military leadership
- Internal coup attempt forcing factional choice

**Typical Timeframe:** 6 months
