# Iran Social State

***Last Updated:** 2026-04-05*

## Current State — Iran Society (as of April 8, 2026)

Iranian society is experiencing simultaneous pressures: a large-scale protest movement (2025-2026), wartime trauma from US-Israel strikes, and the death of the Supreme Leader who had been the symbolic center of the Islamic Republic for 35 years.

**2025-2026 Protest Movement:** Protests triggered by economic collapse (inflation, currency depreciation) began December 28, 2025 and spread to all 31 provinces. The movement is the broadest since Woman Life Freedom (2022), encompassing workers, teachers, pensioners, merchants, students, women, ethnic minorities and rural populations. Chants include "Neither Gaza nor Lebanon, My Life for Iran" — explicitly linking economic grievances to foreign policy priorities.

**Government response:** Extremely violent. Death toll estimates range from 3,117 to higher figures. Internet and telephone access severely restricted since January 8, 2026.

**War impact on society:** US-Israel strikes caused civilian casualties and damage to 120+ historical sites. The war has complicated the protest dynamic — some Iranians rally around the state against external attack; others see the war as consequence of regime policies.

**Valid as of:** April 8, 2026

## Key Actors — Iran Society

**Protest movement (leaderless):** Multi-class coalition without centralized leadership; driven by economic desperation; connects economic and political grievances.

**Women's networks:** Continuing organizational role from Woman Life Freedom movement; integrated into broader coalition.

**Labor movement:** Truck drivers, factory workers, oil sector workers — economically motivated; most sustained organizational base.

**Diaspora:** Growing role in information dissemination; politically fragmented but unified by opposition to the Islamic Republic; Iran International and other Persian-language media.

**Basij:** Mobilized for both domestic repression and war mobilization; under strain from competing demands.

**Clergy (independent):** Some non-regime clerics and marjas have distanced from Mojtaba's succession; Qom dynamics important to watch.

## Trend Analysis — Iran Society

**Trajectory:** The Islamic Republic's social contract is collapsing. The combination of economic failure, political repression, and wartime destruction is generating a legitimacy crisis that may be deeper than previous protest cycles.

**Key dynamic:** The war has created a temporary rally-around-the-flag effect among some segments, complicating the protest movement. But economic deterioration under wartime conditions will intensify grievances over time.

**Demographic pressure:** Youth population remains high but declining (emigration); secularization trend accelerating; public religiosity declining — the regime's ideological base is demographically eroding.

**Near-term risks:** If ceasefire fails and war resumes, social cohesion may fracture rapidly. If ceasefire leads to deal, economic relief could reduce protest intensity without resolving structural grievances.

## Explicit Assumptions — Iran Society (April 8, 2026)

1. **Mass protests ongoing** — All 31 provinces; leaderless multi-class coalition; violent suppression.
2. **Internet severely restricted** — VPN usage widespread but access highly filtered; state controls information environment.
3. **War has not collapsed the protest movement** — Both anti-regime protest and war-related social disruption coexist.
4. **Secularization continuing** — Long-term decline in public religiosity; accelerated by regime's violent responses.
5. **Brain drain accelerating** — Educated youth emigration at record levels; war has increased emigration pressure.
6. **Key uncertainty:** Whether war-rally effect consolidates support for regime or whether economic devastation overwhelms any nationalist sentiment.
