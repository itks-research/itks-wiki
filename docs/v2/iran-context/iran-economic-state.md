# Iran Economic State

***Last Updated:** 2026-04-05*

## Current State — Iran Economy (as of April 8, 2026)

Iran's economy is in wartime collapse. Oil prices rose to ~$100/barrel due to Strait of Hormuz threats during the 39-day war (from ~$70/barrel pre-war), but Iran itself has been unable to export due to sanctions and war conditions. The currency has experienced crisis-level depreciation. Inflation, already at 40-60% before the war, is estimated at 60-100%+ under wartime conditions.

The Strait of Hormuz was threatened/disrupted during the conflict, triggering a global energy crisis. As of April 7-8, ceasefire terms require Iran to reopen the Strait; first ships have passed with Iranian coordination required. This gives Iran substantial leverage in negotiations (Strait passage remains conditional).

Bonyad (parastatal foundation) system remains operational but is severely disrupted by infrastructure strikes. IRGC economic holdings face significant damage.

**Sanctions:** Comprehensive US-EU sanctions remain in force. Iran has demanded full sanctions lifting plus release of frozen assets plus reparations as conditions for any deal.

**Valid as of:** April 8, 2026

## Key Actors — Iran Economy

**IRGC Economic Apparatus** — Controls 15-25% of GDP through bonyads, construction companies, import monopolies. Severely disrupted by strikes but structurally intact.

**Central Bank of Iran** — Managing currency crisis; limited tools under sanctions.

**Bonyad Mostazafin and Setad** — Supreme Leader-controlled foundations; significant assets; continuity under Mojtaba uncertain.

**China** — Primary trading partner and sanctions-buster; relationship continues but strained by war uncertainty; Chinese shipping companies paused Hormuz transits during conflict.

**Pakistan (mediator)** — Hosting ceasefire talks; potential economic corridor partner if sanctions ease.

**Oil Ministry / NIOC** — Oil exports severely curtailed; capacity to resume exports depends on ceasefire-to-deal timeline.

## Trend Analysis — Iran Economy

**Trajectory:** Catastrophic decline. War has compounded years of sanctions-driven contraction. Infrastructure damage from strikes adds to structural weakness. Popular economic grievances (the driver of 2025-2026 protests) have intensified.

**Near-term scenarios:**
- **Deal scenario:** US-Iran agreement lifting sanctions could release frozen assets ($10-20B) and restore oil exports; would stabilize but not quickly repair the economy.
- **Stalemate scenario:** Ceasefire holds but no deal; continued sanctions + war damage = continued collapse; intensified protests.
- **Renewed war scenario:** Ceasefire collapses; oil shock resumes; economy in freefall; possible regime destabilization.

**Structural issues persist regardless:** IRGC economic dominance, bonyad inefficiency, banking sector exclusion from SWIFT, brain drain — all will require structural reform in any post-war scenario.

## Explicit Assumptions — Iran Economy (April 8, 2026)

1. **Oil prices remain elevated (~$100/barrel)** — Strait of Hormuz reopening is fragile; prices reflect risk premium.
2. **Sanctions fully in force** — No sanctions relief has been agreed; ceasefire does not include economic measures.
3. **Currency in crisis** — Rial has collapsed further under wartime conditions; specific rate uncertain.
4. **IRGC economic holdings damaged but intact** — Strikes hit military-industrial complex; bonyad civilian operations partially disrupted.
5. **Frozen assets ($10-20B) remain key negotiating chip** — Iran demands their release; US holds as leverage.
6. **Key uncertainty:** Whether ceasefire leads to sanctions-lifting deal within weeks or months; this is the primary economic variable.
