# Velayat-e Faqih & Clerical Authority

***Last Updated:** 2026-04-05*

## Current State — Velayat-e Faqih & Clerical Authority (as of April 8, 2026)

The Velayat-e Faqih (Guardianship of the Islamic Jurist) system faces its most severe legitimacy crisis since its founding. Ali Khamenei's assassination on February 28, 2026 removed the person who embodied the system's authority for 35 years.

**Mojtaba Khamenei's legitimacy deficit:** Unlike Khomeini (Grand Ayatollah, revolutionary leader) or Ali Khamenei (who gradually consolidated clerical credentials), Mojtaba is a mid-ranking cleric without independent clerical accomplishment. His election by the Assembly of Experts reflects IRGC and hardline political preferences more than theological consensus.

**Marja' challenge:** Major marjas in Qom have notably not uniformly endorsed Mojtaba's election. The separation between political authority (Velayat-e Faqih) and theological authority (marjas followed for religious guidance) — already wide under Ali Khamenei — has widened further.

**Constitutional framework:** The Velayat-e Faqih article (Article 5) requires the Supreme Leader to be a faqih (jurist) with qualities including justice, piety, political and social perspicacity, and administrative ability. Mojtaba's selection stretches these requirements.

**Valid as of:** April 8, 2026

## Key Actors — Velayat-e Faqih System

**Mojtaba Khamenei** — Third Supreme Leader; son of Ali Khamenei; elected March 9, 2026. Legitimacy rests on IRGC backing and dynastic continuity rather than clerical consensus.

**Assembly of Experts** — Elected Mojtaba; played constitutionally correct role; but election reflects political realities more than theological deliberation.

**Qom marjas** — Grand Ayatollahs (Sistani in Najaf, Makarem Shirazi, Safi Golpaygani, etc.) — relationship to Mojtaba's authority unclear; some distancing observed.

**Ahmad Jannati (Guardian Council)** — Elder hardliner; key legitimizing figure.

**Hossein-Ali Montazeri legacy** — Montazeri's model of quietist Shia authority (marjas should not govern) has gained retroactive credibility as Velayat-e Faqih faces crisis.

**IRGC** — The real power behind Mojtaba's succession; theological legitimacy replaced by coercive legitimacy.

## Trend Analysis — Velayat-e Faqih

**Trajectory:** The theological legitimacy of Velayat-e Faqih is declining sharply. The combination of:
- Khamenei's assassination (undermining the Supreme Leader's inviolability)
- Dynastic succession (contradicts the meritocratic clerical principle)
- IRGC dominance (reveals who actually holds power)
- Declining public religiosity (reduces the religious appeal of clerical governance)
...represents a cumulative delegitimization that may be irreversible.

**Key question for ITKS:** Does the Velayat-e Faqih system collapse from within (clerical rejection), from without (military coup or popular revolution), or does it adapt (become a rubber stamp for IRGC rule)?

**Near-term:** Mojtaba will likely try to consolidate through successful ceasefire deal — a peace deal would give him a political victory his father never achieved. Failure would accelerate his delegitimization.

**Historical parallel:** The Islamic Republic's trajectory resembles post-revolutionary states where ideological founding principles erode under the pressure of institutional survival — the question is what replaces them.

## Explicit Assumptions — Velayat-e Faqih (April 8, 2026)

1. **Mojtaba Khamenei is Supreme Leader** — Legally elected by Assembly of Experts; constitutionally valid but theologically contested.
2. **IRGC is the power behind the throne** — Mojtaba's authority depends on IRGC loyalty; no independent clerical or popular base.
3. **Qom is not uniformly behind Mojtaba** — Notable silence or distancing from major marjas; separation of religious and political authority widening.
4. **Velayat-e Faqih system intact structurally** — Guardian Council, Expediency Council operational; constitutional mechanisms functioning.
5. **Public religiosity declining** — Long-term secularization trend accelerated by repression and economic failure.
6. **Key uncertainty:** Whether Mojtaba can establish independent legitimacy through successful governance/diplomacy, or whether the succession crisis becomes a permanent legitimacy wound.
