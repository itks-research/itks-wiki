# Methodology

## What Is ITKS?

The **Iran Development Knowledge System** (ITKS) is an autonomous AI-driven research platform that maps development pathways relevant to Iran's future governance. It continuously discovers, indexes, scores, and synthesizes academic and policy sources from global repositories.

The system does not advocate for any specific governance model. It evaluates the full spectrum of evidence — measuring what research suggests would best deliver prosperity, personal freedoms, economic strength, and sovereignty for Iran.

## How Sources Are Collected

ITKS queries two major academic APIs:

- **Semantic Scholar** — access to 200M+ papers with citation graphs
- **OpenAlex** — open scholarly metadata covering journals, conferences, and preprints

Each research cycle begins with **directives** — targeted search tasks specifying a case study and category (e.g., "Find sources about transitional justice in Tunisia"). The engine generates multiple search queries per directive, deduplicates results against existing records, and stores new sources.

## How Scoring Works

Every source passes through a multi-layer evaluation pipeline:

| Layer | Process | Output |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **Layer 2A** | Relevance scoring (1-5 scale), category assignment, case study verification | Scored, classified sources |
| **Layer 2B** | Relationship detection between sources (supports, contradicts, extends, shared methodology) | Knowledge graph edges |
| **Layer 2C** | Synthesis article generation from clusters of high-relevance sources | Thematic analysis articles |

All processing uses local AI models (no data leaves the system). Sources that score below confidence thresholds are flagged for human review.

## Case Study Selection

Eight countries were selected as comparative reference points for Iran:

| Country | Rationale |
|---------|-----------|
| **South Korea** | Military-to-democratic transition, economic transformation |
| **Spain** | Post-authoritarian consensus model, regional autonomy management |
| **Tunisia** | Arab Spring democratic transition, religion-state negotiation |
| **Poland** | Post-communist transformation, EU integration pathway |
| **Indonesia** | Military economic divestment, multi-ethnic governance |
| **Chile** | Constitutional reform processes, economic liberalization |
| **Czech Republic** | Lustration models, privatization approaches |
| **Iran** | Primary subject — unique challenges of post-theocratic transition |

## How Synthesis Articles Are Generated

Synthesis articles are evidence-based analyses that draw from clusters of scored sources. Each article follows a structured format:

1. **Key Insights** — The most important findings with direct relevance to Iran
2. **What We Know** — Evidence-backed patterns from comparative cases
3. **What We Don't Know** — Open questions and research gaps
4. **Sub-Articles** — Focused deep-dives on specific dimensions

Articles are generated by AI and reviewed for accuracy. Every claim traces to a verifiable source.

## Content Status Badges

| Badge | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Machine Generated | Initial AI-generated content, not yet reviewed |
| AI Reviewed | Content reviewed and refined by AI quality checks |
| Human Reviewed | Content verified by a human researcher |
| Human Modified | Content edited directly by a human researcher |

## Farsi Support

All content is available in Farsi (Persian). Farsi translations are generated using a three-pass methodology:

1. **Meaning extraction** — key points extracted in English
2. **Farsi composition** — fresh writing in Farsi using a controlled glossary
3. **Verification** — checked for accuracy, natural syntax, and glossary compliance

## Limitations

- All sources are machine-scored; human review is ongoing but incomplete
- Synthesis articles reflect available evidence, not policy recommendations
- Some comparative cases have stronger coverage than others
- Iran-specific categories (IRGC, bonyads, seminary system) have limited comparative precedent
- The system is continuously updated; findings may change as new evidence is incorporated
