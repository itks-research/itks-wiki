Despite recent assertions that he rose from a lower middle-class background,' his family was actually quite prominent in the Iranian military; his father headed the Tehran Military Academy created by the early twentieth-century reformer Mushir alDowleh. Haj Ali attended a French school in Tehran. After graduating from the Military Academy he joined the Cossack Division. Razmara's background was a privileged one, but his career was fueled by intelligence and initiative. In his early twenties, he tied his fortunes to those of the head of the Division Cossack (and later Shah) Reza Khan, which assured him a place of some prominence in the Iranian military. Following Reza Khan's 1921 coup d'etat, Razmara participated in the suppression of the Gilan Soviet Republic. He later took part in suppressing Kurdish tribal revolts in western Azerbaijan.2 Haj Ali's performance in these conflicts was distinguished enough to attract Reza Khan's attention; Razmara was one of the first officers sent to St Cyr.
