## چکیده

Objective: The development of Women-Friendly and Child-Caring Villages (DRPPA) in Indonesia has not been implemented optimally. This study aims to identify the factors that influence the success of DRPPA development in Indonesia, aligning with the Sustainable Development Goal 5 (SDG 5) on Gender Equality, which emphasizes the importance of empowering women and promoting their rights in order to achieve equitable and inclusive community development.
 
Theoretical Framework: The study is grounded in the understanding that various interrelated factors contribute to the effectiveness of community empowerment initiatives, particularly in the context of developing women-friendly and child-caring environments.
 
Method: This study employs a quantitative approach using Exploratory Factor Analysis (EFA) to identify the main factors that influence the success of DRPPA development, specifically in the provinces of West Java, Yogyakarta, and Bali, which are the pilot project areas for DRPPA.
 
Results and Discussion: From the analysis of 30 tested indicators, five dominant factors were identified that significantly impact the implementation of DRPPA: Village Social Infrastructure, Stakeholders, Culture, Policy, and Resources. Village Social Infrastructure factors encompass efforts to enhance community capacity and access to services. Stakeholders reflect the role of relevant parties in supporting the program. Culture illustrates the influence of local values on the program's success. Policy emphasizes the necessity of inclusive and empowering policies, while Resources refer to the availability of funds, labor, and facilities that support DRPPA implementation.
 
Research Implications: The findings indicate the need for a multi-faceted approach to successfully implement DRPPA, highlighting the importance of collaboration among stakeholders, cultural sensitivity, and resource allocation.
 
Originality/Value: This study contributes to the literature on community empowerment and development by providing a comprehensive analysis of the factors influencing DRPPA success in Indonesia, which can inform policy and practice in similar contexts.
