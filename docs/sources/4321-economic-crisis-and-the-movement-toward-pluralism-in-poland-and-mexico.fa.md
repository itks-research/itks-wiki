## چکیده

The historical development of politics in Mexico and Poland, their respective current socioeconomic situations, and the roles that they have traditionally played in the international arena invite a comparative analysis of the roots of the political liberalization process that has unfolded in both nations. Pioneering steps in establishing a conceptual framework for the comparison between Mexico and Poland were taken by Jeffrey Hughes, who through a broad historical, cultural and socioeconomic view examined the dependent status of both nations.1 This article compares the pattern of interaction between economic and political factors that led to the movement toward political pluralism and liberalization in both societies by the 1980s. While the detail may vary, the general politicoeconomic trend in both societies is largely analogous and comparable, and it stemmed principally from elite perceptions of the political requisites of economic liberalization.
