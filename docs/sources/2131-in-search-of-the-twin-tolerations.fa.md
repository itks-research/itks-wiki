## چکیده

Abstract U.S. foreign policy should encourage regimes to foster the twin tolerations, permitting full religious practice and limiting religion's control of government. Political theology and differentiation (the degree of separation between church and state)determine why religious actors take particular forms of politics. Religious terrorist groups are more likely to originate in integrationist regimes, where there is low differentiation, where religious moderates are co-opted, and where more traditional believers are denied political participation. Examples include Egypt, Algeria, and the Shah's Iran. Democratization movements within Islam are characterized by a political ideology with an institutional separation between religion and state.
