## Abstract

BACKGROUND
In Spain the transfer of nurse education to universities was accompanied by a shift towards a model of person-centred care.


AIM
To explore whether the change in nurses' professional profile (from physician assistant to providers of person-centred care) was a response to changing needs in Spanish society.


DESIGN
Qualitative study.


METHODS
Theoretical sampling and in-depth interviews using an inductive analytical approach.


RESULTS
Four categories described the nursing profession in Spain prior to the introduction of university training: the era of medical assistants; technologisation of hospitals; personal care of the patient based on Christian values; professional socialisation differentiated by gender. Further analysis showed that these categories could be subsumed under a broader core category: the transfer of nurse education to universities as part of Spain's transition to democracy.


CONCLUSION
The transfer of nurse education to universities was one of several changes occurring in Spanish society during the country's transition to democratic government. The redefined public health system required a highly skilled workforce, with improved employment rights being given to female health professionals, notably nurses.
