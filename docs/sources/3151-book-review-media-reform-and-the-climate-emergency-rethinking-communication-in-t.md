## Abstract

As she puts it, “Gawker’s rejection of the trappings of traditional journalistic professionalism may have made staffers more readily able to identify as workers.” Scholars, students, and journalists alike will learn a great deal from the nuanced and probing picture this book gives us of work in the contemporary digital newsroom. It’s important to note, of course, that the experience of workplace metrics will vary in other media systems, and in less iconic, local, or chain-run US outlets, especially as layoffs and cutbacks continue. Petre’s absorbing account offers a clear roadmap for future studies of the rationalization of labor in digital journalism.
