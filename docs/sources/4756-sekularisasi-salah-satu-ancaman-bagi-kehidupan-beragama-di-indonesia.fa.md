## چکیده

In this day and age where we are all in the era of modernization, now the majority of people have understood the concept of secularism and secularization. In this study, researchers discuss the phenomenon of secularization in Indonesia and its threat to religious life. Secularization is defined as the process of separation between religion and the state, where religion no longer has a central role in public and political life. Many people say that religion is the opium of the world and should be separated from public and political life. This article argues that secularization can be a threat to religious life in Indonesia for several reasons. Secularism should be seen as a constructive criticism for Indonesia. Secularism can lead to moral decline, but it also encourages reflection and strengthens Pancasila as the basis of the state. The research method used in this research uses a literature study, namely by searching and analyzing data and information sources related to secularism. In this study, formulation of the problem researchers discussed the process of secularization in Indonesia, the threat of secularization for religious life in Indonesia, and how religious people in Indonesia respond to this issue. This article aims to concludes that secularization is a complex phenomenon with various impacts on religious life in Indonesia. The result is religious people in Indonesia need to understand secularization well and respond to it wisely in order to maintain a healthy and tolerant religious life.
