## چکیده

This chapter approaches the relationships between curriculum reforms and cultural values of countries or regions. The cases considered correspond to Italy and its most recent reform movement (2001–2018), with the implementation of ‘mathematical laboratories’; Serbia and the changes operated about the nature of mathematics as a teaching subject (1970–1985); and Iran since the establishment of a formal educational system in 1920. The chapter illustrates how driving forces and barriers that are not directly of a mathematical nature – but rather political, economic or social – can explain many of the changes that occur within educational systems and the way these changes are concretely operated.
