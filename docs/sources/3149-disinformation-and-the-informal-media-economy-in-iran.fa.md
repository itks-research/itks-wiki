## چکیده

abstract:Recent research on disinformation has largely focused on the role of formal institutions. In contrast, this article draws on the case of Iran to theorize the informal economy and its contribution to state disinformation campaigns. Scholars often emphasize legacy media's role in amplifying the credibility of disinformation. In Iran, however, broadcast media are controlled by the state, holding little credibility with audiences. Instead, the informal circulation of media is a trusted means of accessing information and culture. This article, by studying the informal circulation of media, shows how the values projected onto informal systems can actually help sustain disinformation efforts.
