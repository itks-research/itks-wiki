## Abstract

This paper explores the relationship between women’s autonomy and reproductive behavior in four selected provinces of Iran, namely Gilan, West Azarbaijan, Sistan Baluchistan and Yazd. Data for this study comes from the 2002 Iran Fertility Transition Survey (IFTS) which was conducted in the four selected provinces, and collected information on various aspects of reproductive health as well as several dimensions of women’s autonomy from around 5,190 married women of reproductive ages. The paper will, first, review the literature on the relationship between women’s autonomy and reproductive behavior, present the data used, and also briefly describe the research setting. Second, the status and autonomy of women in pre- and post-revolutionary Iran as well as the background to the study will be reviewed. The discussion will then be followed by an analysis of the characteristics as well as the autonomy indicators in the four selected provinces. Finally, we will present the result of multivariate analysis on the relationship between women’s autonomy and reproductive behavior (contraceptive use and fertility). The results show that provinces differ in terms of women’s autonomy, fertility, as well as contraceptive use. Women in Sistan Baluchistan and West Azarbaijan have lower autonomy than those in Gilan and Yazd. Our finding indicates the relationship between women’s autonomy and reproductive behavior. When other socio-economic and demographic characteristics were controlled, women’s autonomy was significantly related to fertility and contraceptive use. Education was found the most important variable predicting fertility and contraceptive use. However, it seems that socio-cultural context of the four provinces have had significant impact upon both women’s autonomy and reproductive behavior. More qualitative research is needed to explore the interplay of ethnicity, religion and reproductive behavior in the selected provinces.
