## Abstract

This study explores the relationship between presidential communication and press freedom in Ghana, focusing on President John Dramani Mahama’s 2025 engagements with journalists. Specifically, it examines two key events: the September 5, 2025 closed-door meeting with the Ghana Journalists Association (GJA) and the September 10, 2025 televised media interaction. Using Fairclough's critical discourse analysis framework, the research analyzes Mahama's rhetoric on media freedom, accountability, and transparency within Ghana’s complex democratic and media landscape. Data were collected from digital media platforms: GhanaWeb, Graphic Online, MyJoyOnline, GBC Ghana Online, presidential statements, and post-engagement commentaries. Structured interviews with journalists and live broadcast observations were also included. The findings highlight a gap between symbolic and substantive accountability, with rhetoric often outpacing actual reforms. Despite Mahama’s emphasis on transparency and journalist safety, his statements did not align with tangible changes. This study deepens our understanding of how presidential discourse shapes media freedom in Ghana’s evolving democracy.
