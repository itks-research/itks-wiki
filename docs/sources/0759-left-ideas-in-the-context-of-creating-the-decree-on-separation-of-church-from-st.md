## Abstract

There are many studies in Russia about the Decree on the Separation of Church and State and School from Church, but mostly about how the provisions of this decree were implemented in different parts of the country. The decree itself, how it appeared, who its creator was, the reasons for its appearance and the legal idea as a whole remained on the sidelines. The document became an important element in the story of the confrontation between the church and the Soviet state. This article examines the French experience in creating the Soviet decree, as well as how the Esers ideas on the separation of church and state, which were softer and more flexible than the Bolshevik ones, are connected with the decree. The article does not answer questions about the degree of influence of European left anticlerical ideas and Esers ideas on the decree, but bring up a questions about the facets of the lack of study of the document, to which so many scientific works are devoted.
