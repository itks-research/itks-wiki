## Abstract

After 1918, Poland faced the challenge of repealing partitioning powers’ regulations and implementing new laws to shape the agricultural system. Key legislative actions included the agrarian reform laws of 1919, 1920, and 1925, which focused on partitioning large estates and creating or expanding peasant farms. The 1921 Constitution regulated land ownership, emphasizing state control over land transactions. During the interwar period, significant efforts were made to systematize agrarian law, culminating in the Draft Agrarian Code of 1928, authored by Władysław Leopold Jaworski. This code aimed to restructure the agricultural sector, combining public law regulation with material and formal parts, and introducing new institutions such as the agricultural enterprise.Post-World War II, Poland adopted a socialist system, characterized by administrative regulation of agrarian relations and significant state intervention. The period from 1945 to 1955 saw accelerated collectivization, with agrarian law serving as an instrument for socialist restructuring. The scope of agrarian legal regulation broadened to include various aspects of agricultural production and market organization. In the 1970s, another attempt was made to codify agricultural law, reflecting a different socio-political reality. The legislation included both civil and administrative regulations, with the Civil Code of 1964 incorporating specific agricultural provisions. The period until 1980 saw a return to civil law regulations, aiming to prevent farm fragmentation and balance civil and administrative regulations. The system transformation after 1989 led to the implementation of social-market economy principles. This period involved privatization, restructuring, and liberalization of agricultural land transactions, with aspirations for European Union integration becoming apparent. EU agricultural legislation has since become a dominant source of agricultural law in Poland. The ongoing discussion about creating an Agricultural Code in Poland, announced in December 2022, aims to comprehensively regulate agricultural activities, rural development, and farmers’ rights. The proposed code includes tasks for public authorities, protection principles for agricultural activities, and mechanisms for crisis support and fair trade practices. In conclusion, the evolution of agricultural law in Poland reflects significant shifts from administrative to civil law regulation, influenced by socio-political changes and integration with EU policies.
