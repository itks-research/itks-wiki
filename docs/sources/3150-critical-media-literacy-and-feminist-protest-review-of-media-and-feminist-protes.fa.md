## چکیده

Media and Feminist Protest in Iran: My Camera is My Weapon by May (2024) explores the critical role of media, especially digital platforms, in mobilizing feminist protests within Iran. May’s research provides an in-depth analysis of how the Iranian Women’s Movement (IWM) and other feminist movements have used social media, particularly Twitter (now X), to resist gender-based oppression and discriminatory policies in the Islamic Republic of Iran (IRI). These movements have used technology as a tool for mobilization to circumvent censorship and state control, advancing their agenda for gender equality May’s work highlights the contemporary role of media in activism while tracing its historical roots, offering a comprehensive understanding of the Iranian feminist struggle. The book is divided into several thematic chapters that discuss social media activism, the Iranian women’s rights movement, and how the digital age has influenced political and cultural activism. She begins by exploring the relationship between social media and resistance in authoritarian regimes, analyzing how platforms like Twitter have become essential for organizing, communicating, and spreading awareness of social movements. Hashtags, memes, and user-generated content are presented as powerful strategies for collective mobilization in the digital age, enabling the rapid spread of information across borders. The Iranian Women’s Movement, as analysed in the book, serves as a case study to demonstrate the ways in which feminist protest has evolved in response to increasing digital access.
