## Abstract

The paper contains a short account of the unification of Polish civil law, a task that was&#13;\naccomplished in 1964 with the adoption of the civil code and the family and guardianship code. Those&#13;\ncodifications, rooted in the communist system, were substantially modified in 1989. However, despite&#13;\nover 50 amendments, certain elements of the communist doctrine have remained in both codes till&#13;\ntoday. Therefore further modifications are necessary and will have to be incorporated in a new code of&#13;\ncivil law, together with the new system of values set out in the Constitution of 1997 as well as the&#13;\nCommunity laws that Poland is also required to account for in its legal system. Work on the drafting of&#13;\na new legislative bill has already commenced at the Civil Law Codification Committee at the Ministry&#13;\nof Justice.
