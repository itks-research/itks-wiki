## Abstract

This paper discusses modern approaches to the organization and holding of Annual general meetings of shareholders in Ukraine. The conclusion is made about the exceptional importance of Annual general meetings in the system of management bodies of a joint stock company and the current state of legal regulation of holding Annual general meetings under Ukrainian legislation. 
A comparative legal description of the international practice of organizing Annual general meetings in the United Kingdom, Germany, France and Poland has been carried out. The general trends in the digitalization of corporate governance of a joint stock company, quorum requirements, forms of participation, voting rules and transparency of decision-making have been highlighted. The features of the cancellation, invalidating and annulment of Annual general meetings in the United Kingdom, Germany, France and Poland have been characterized. 
The specifics of holding Annual general meetings in person, remote and electronically are described. Special attention is paid to novelties in the organization of activities, the procedure for holding and the powers of Annual general meetings under martial law in Ukraine. The main innovations related to the introduction of electronic and remote forms of shareholder participation in meetings, as well as the procedural features of their holding under martial law, are highlighted. 
The results obtained allowed us to identify certain shortcomings in the legal regulation of general meetings of joint stock companies in Ukraine, namely: limited detailing of the rights of minority shareholders; lack of clear guarantees of transparency of electronic meetings; insufficient development of feedback and discussion mechanisms; limited influence of shareholders on management remuneration; lack of an electronic participation mechanism without the mediation of a depository; insufficient regulation of the hybrid form of meetings and the lack of effective sanctions for violations of the meeting procedure. The identified shortcomings allowed us to outline the prospects for the development of Ukrainian legislation on the legal regulation of general meetings of joint stock companies.
