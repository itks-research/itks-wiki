## چکیده

Abstract David Ost: Beginning in 1993, former communist parties, in their new “social democratic” guises, began returning to power in Eastern Europe. First in Lithuania, then in Poland and Hungary, the victories of ex-communists raised some concerns about the possible return of the ancien regime. Far from bringing about any “return to communism,” however, the ex-communists quickly made clear their full commitment to the transition to capitalism. Showing themselves true to their new name, these social democratic parties have continued the path of marketization and privatization, with symbolic tripartite commissions, cost-of-living increases to retirees and phraseology about “social justice” offered as the only consolations.
