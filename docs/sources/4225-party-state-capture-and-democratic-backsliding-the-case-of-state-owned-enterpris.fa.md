## چکیده

ABSTRACT Democratic backsliding leads to party state capture, which is particularly evident in the case of some Central and Eastern European countries. State-owned enterprises are a public resource that politicians are eager to capture. However, there is a lack of explanation as to how this phenomenon manifests in practice and what processes lead to it. This article investigates whether and how these processes occur using the case of Poland as the biggest country of the European Union in which democratic backsliding occurred after 2015. By conducting in-depth interviews with 20 CEOs, as well as management and supervisory board members of Polish SOEs, I explore whether – and if so, how – state-owned enterprises were used by politicians as a tool for the reproduction of power during this period. The research found that in the years 2015–21, SOEs were subject to party state capture. I conclude that the reasons for this were: the weakening of the rule of law, the decentralisation of the ownership model, the liberalisation of the requirements for persons employed in the management and supervisory boards of SOEs, the electoral system, and the system of management of the ruling party and the state by its leader.
