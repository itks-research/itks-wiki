## چکیده

This review paper synthesizes contemporary research on the media's role in contexts of war, censorship, and political influence. Drawing from multiple case studies, it explores how media systems operate within democratic and authoritarian frameworks, analyzing the mechanisms of state control, self-censorship, propaganda, visual memory, and public trust. The paper situates these findings within broader debates on journalistic ethics, freedom of expression, and the shifting boundaries of objectivity in the age of mass communication.
