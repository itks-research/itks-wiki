## Abstract

Abdellatif Hermessi, Islamic Fundamentalism and the Tunisian State In deciding to impose social changes from the top, political leaders in Tunisia have upset established beliefs and patterns of socialization. In doing so, they have destabilized the society in a long-lasting way. Although Tunisia has not experienced the conditions that led to the separation between Church and State in some European countries, the neo-Destour party did not hesitate to incorporate the idea in its modernization program. The Islamic movement which was created partially in reaction to it has been strengthened by a fusion of religious and social opposition to these changes.
