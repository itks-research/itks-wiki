Chapter six surveys patterns of social capital and political trust in Turkey, where social capital is defined as ‘social trust’ and ‘social participation’ whereas ‘political trust’ means trust in political institutions and political systems (p. 120). Abbas uses 4272 Turkish respondents between 2004 and 2008 waves of the European Social Survey for his analysis. He argues that the intersectionality of social capital and political trust depends on cultural identity and religion as he unravelled that religious bourgeois Muslims are least likely to trust the EU. Finally, in the conclusion chapter, Abbas asserts that Turkey’s multiple and conflictual transformations in the last twenty-thirty years are still unfolding which are also affecting ‘the nature of the relationship between Islam, democracy and ethnic relations’ (p. 140). He acknowledges the limits of ‘secularism’ versus ‘Islamism’ antagonism in the Turkish setting and he problematizes both concepts. It is argued that the AKP usefully managed to intersect Islam and capitalism and rerationalized political Islam based on market values; therefore, Turkey has become a site for ‘competitive market Islam’. Abbas also claims that ‘[n]ationalism remains resilient in Turkey . . . [and] Erdoğan is . . . both neo-Ottoman and neo-Kemalist’ (p. 150). Therefore, it is safe to argue that the ‘radical new society’ in Turkey is based on an essential normalization of the use of these ideologies. In the postscript, Abbas gave the ‘rhetoric of national unity’ that was constructed by CHP, MHP and AKP based around the demonization of Gulenists and the PKK following the coup-attempt in 2016 as an example of this combined ideology of Islamonationalism (p. 159). All in all, Contemporary Turkey in Conflict: Ethnicity, Islam and Politics is a well-researched book, offering a distinctive analysis of the conflictual Turkish politics in the last fifteen years. It is a clear written book and it is free from academic jargon which makes the book a pleasure to read. Noticeably, the book does not aim the academic circles only, it also aims the general reader interested in contemporary Turkish politics. Contemporary Turkey in Conflict is a noteworthy book in sociology, politics and area studies.
