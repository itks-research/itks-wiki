## چکیده

At its inception, developing an FFP is about the creation of strong structures so that the enforcement of gender-sensitive policy ceases to be dependent on engaged and committed individuals in high level positions. FFP goes to the core of governments and society. It ultimately aims at changing the mentality of people in power and their relation to society – and the relation of societies to governments. In the current political climate of contestation, equality-focused policies can provide a powerful alternative. If applied comprehensively, FFP disrupts the dominant, state security-centred logic of foreign policy to replace it with a human-centred approach. Awareness of a different mindset of “doing things” entailed in FFP, especially in relation to its strong participatory and self-reflective elements, can serve as a tangible and much-needed alternative to the status quo.
