## Abstract

The purpose of this article is to examine the transformation of Iran’s foreign policy in the context of the Russian-Ukrainian war and to analyze its consequences for bilateral relations (Ukrainian-Iranian, Russian-Iranian), as well as for the country’s regional and global positions.The research methodology is based on a systemic approach that enables the integration of various facts and trends into a comprehensive picture of the evolution of Iran's foreign policy under the influence of the Russian-Ukrainian war. The study applies problem-chronological, structural-functional, and informational approaches, along with the method of comparative analysis.The article’s scientific novelty lies in the comprehensive analysis of the changes that have occurred in Iran’s international standing and foreign policy following Russia’s full-scale aggression against Ukraine, launched on February 24, 2022. The article attempts to identify the areas of Iran’s foreign policy that have undergone the most significant changes specifically due to the war in Ukraine. Conclusions. Despite the active use of ideological politico-religious slogans in public discourse, in practice, the Iranian leadership demonstrates political pragmatism aimed at advancing the interests of the ruling clerical regime. These interests, in our opinion, do not coincide with the national interests of the Iranian state and society, as they lead to the preservation of Iran’s sanctioned status, further deterioration of the economic situation, isolation from advanced technologies, weakening of regional influence, increasing confrontation with powerful regional actors, the absence of reliable allies, and the erosion of international reputation. Iran’s direct involvement in the Russian-Ukrainian war confirms the relevance of Oriental studies, and in particular Iranian studies, in Ukraine, for the development of effective foreign policy, the strengthening of international standing, and the prevention of external threats.
