## Abstract

This chapter analyses the urban participation function and pattern in Iran, following the shift in 1997. While the shift in urban governance plays a significant part in the decentralisation process occurring in Iran, it is becoming increasingly difficult to ignore the complexities inherent in the transition and the practices that it introduced. Neighbourhood councils are newly established bodies that, together with local civic associations, operate in the bottom-most sphere of city governance and provide a fresh participatory governance space at local level. The interviews with municipality officials and local councillors as well as the group of experts indicate that the shift and the establishment of the Islamic City Council (ICC) have had an effect on four governance areas: decentralisation and local autonomy, accountability and transparency, gender equality in city governance and selection of the mayor. The establishment of the ICC was part of the reform and the decentralisation of powers to local institutions and citizens.
