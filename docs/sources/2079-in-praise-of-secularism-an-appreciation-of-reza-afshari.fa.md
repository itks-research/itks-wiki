## چکیده

Since the Islamic Revolution of 1979, members of the Iranian Baha’i community have been persecuted. The Baha’i are followers of the nineteenth century Persian prophet, Bah’u’lláh, whom they consider a messenger of God along with Jesus, Muhammad, and others. An estimated 200 Baha’i were executed in Iran from 1979 to 1984.1 To this day Baha’i endure considerable political and legal disabilities, such as exclusion from universities and frequent harassment and imprisonment. Many thousands are now in exile, having suffered grievously from torture, imprisonment, deprivation of property, exclusion from their professions, and execution of family members.2
