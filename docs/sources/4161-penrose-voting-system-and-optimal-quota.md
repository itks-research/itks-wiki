The a priori votingpower of a voter measures his potential in˛uence over the decisions of thevoting body under a given decision rule. We investigate a system based onthe law of Penrose, in which each representativein the voting body receivesthe number of votes (the voting weight) proportional to the square root ofthe population he or she represents. Here we demonstrate that for a genericdistribution of the population there exists an optimal quota for which thevoting power of any state is proportional to its weight.
