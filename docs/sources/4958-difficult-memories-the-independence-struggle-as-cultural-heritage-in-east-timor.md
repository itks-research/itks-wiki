## Abstract

In Timor-Leste, these difficult legacies are complicated by the distinct cultural 
and linguistic affiliations promoted by successive colonial regimes, political 
schisms within the former independence movement, a lack justice for the victims 
of human rights abuses during the Indonesian occupation, and the recent rise of 
regional tensions. ese fissures have complicated the process of nation-building, 
and the articulation of a unifying postcolonial national identity. As such, they are 
critical to understanding the cultural heritage of the independence struggle and 
its conservation in Timor-Leste, which is itself an exercise in articulating cultural 
nationalism.
