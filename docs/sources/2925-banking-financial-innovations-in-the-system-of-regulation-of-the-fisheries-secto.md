## Abstract

The article provides a comprehensive study of the role of banking financial innovations in the system of regulation of the fisheries sector and in shaping a competitive aquaculture industry in Ukraine under conditions of financial market globalization and extraordinary challenges. The relevance of the research is driven by the deepening systemic transformations of the national economy caused by military threats, financial instability, logistical constraints, and the growing dependence of the domestic market on imported resources, which intensifies the risks to strategically important branches of the food sector, particularly fisheries. It is substantiated that the current model of regulation of Ukraine’s fisheries sector is characterized by the limited adaptability of traditional mechanisms of public financing and credit support to conditions of high uncertainty and extraordinary challenges. In this context, banking financial innovations are considered a system-forming element of the sector’s institutional transformation, shaping a new architecture of interaction among banking institutions, public authorities, and business entities in fisheries and aquaculture. The article reveals the economic substance and functional role of banking financial innovations as a set of digital banking products, fintech solutions, instruments of project-based and program-targeted financing, «green» and «blue» loans, as well as risk-oriented models of lending and financial monitoring. It is demonstrated that the implementation of these instruments enhances aquaculture enterprises’ access to financial resources, optimizes financial flows, reduces sector-specific risks, and increases the transparency of financial and economic processes. Special attention is paid to the sectoral specificity of fisheries and aquaculture, which combines economic, biological, and environmental factors and necessitates specialized financial instruments adapted to production seasonality, long investment cycles, and the heightened risk profile of economic activity. It is argued that banking financial innovations perform an adaptive function by aligning financial regulation 
with the technological and natural-climatic characteristics of the sector. A scientific and methodological approach is proposed to formalize the impact of banking financial innovations on the competitiveness of fisheries and aquaculture through the use of an integral indicator that accounts for the level of financial innovation adoption, the volume of investments in resource-efficient and technologically innovative solutions, environmental performance, and the institutional capacity of the regulatory environment. It is proven that banking financial innovations exert a multiplicative effect on sectoral development by simultaneously stimulating investment activity, reducing financial and environmental risks, and strengthening the integration of Ukrainian aquaculture into global value chains. Within the framework of the principles of the circular and blue economy, it is substantiated that banking financial innovations facilitate the transition from a linear financing model to a cyclical financial-investment model of development oriented toward resource reuse, reduction of biological raw material losses, restoration of aquatic ecosystems, and increased environmental responsibility of production. This creates the preconditions for the formation of long-term competitive advantages for Ukrainian aquaculture in domestic and international markets. The practical significance of the results lies in their applicability to the design and implementation of public policies for fisheries regulation, aquaculture support programs, and the activities of banking institutions and business entities aimed at enhancing the sector’s financial resilience, ensuring food security, and achieving the goals of sustainable, circular, and blue development of the national economy.
