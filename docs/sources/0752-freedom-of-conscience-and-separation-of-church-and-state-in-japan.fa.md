## چکیده

The article examines the process of formation and consolidation of the concepts of “religion”, “religious
association”, “religious activity” in the legislation and judicial practice of Japan. The problem of defining
and limiting freedom of conscience and religion is considered in two aspects — as a subjective right of an individual
and as a prohibition on public authorities to exercise authority based on religious views. The problem of demarcation
of religious and ordinary regulation is also touched upon, taking into account the specifics of the national identity
of the Japanese.
