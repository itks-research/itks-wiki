## Abstract

Iran as a very ancient country is experiencing a different kind of media policy that is<br>based on freedom, religion and social responsibility. A general look at the media in Iran<br>shows that during the period after the Islamic Revolution considerable development had<br>taken place in the area of media freedom. A wide range of press media is currently<br>available in Iran. Because of high attentions to the radio and TV as a national university,<br>it remains the most popular medium. In terms of online media, Iran is one of few<br>countries in the world where the new form of journalism - online journalism - and a new<br>type of media – online only news organizations - have received serious attention and<br>development in the last 9 years. Iran has the largest number of blogs in the world after<br>China and the online internet media in Iran is going to be as a mass media. However,<br>while the Iranian press has displayed great diversity and vibrancy, some basic legal<br>safeguards for media freedom are still lacking. After 30 years of Islamic Revolution, the<br>press remains vulnerable. In spite of a lot of improvement in media legislation, media<br>freedom is still an issue and there are several intermixed legal and educational obstacles<br>that directly or indirectly damage the development of the media freedom. These obstacles<br>are includes; decreasing professional standards of journalism, the complexity of new<br>media and the lack of updating media legislation, the limitation or monopoly of media<br>publication, the independence of the Judiciary, the lack of clear definition of “Media<br>offense” and “Media court” in the law, and unbalanced distribution of government<br>subsidies to the press. For further development and toward desirable climate for media<br>freedom in Iran, a lot of decisions need to be made to address these obstacles, specially in<br>terms of legislation and Education of Journalists. <br>
