## چکیده

This collection of papers is the product of a conference sponsored by Westminster<br> and Koeceli Universities. The topic of globalisation, religion and development<br> sparked much interest and debate prompting the conference organisers to<br> make a selection of the conference papers available in printed form. On behalf<br> of those who attended the conference I would like to thank Dr. Farhang Morady<br> and Dr. Ismail Siriner for their hard work in setting up the conference and<br> in bringing this collection to fruition. The papers have been arranged in three<br> sections: Globalisation, Religion and Development; Gender and Development;<br> and Case Studies in Development.
