Iran's current electoral architecture operates under fundamentally different constraints. The نظارت استصوابی system, documented in studies of the Guardian Council's گزینش و بررسی صلاحیت practices (Tezcur, 2005), means that electoral rules matter less than candidate filtration — the game is decided before voters enter the booth. Kazemi and Akhzarian Kashani (2023) examine Iran's electoral حکمرانی model comparatively, finding that it lacks the institutional independence that even flawed democracies maintain between election administration and political power.
